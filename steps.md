
# 1️⃣ Raw Material Testing (Incoming QC)

Before production starts, QC verifies that all materials meet specifications.

### Typical Materials

* Cell culture media
* Buffers and salts
* Resins (Protein A, ion exchange)
* Excipients
* Water (WFI)

### Tests Performed

* Identity (FTIR, NIR, HPLC)
* Purity
* Bioburden
* Endotoxin (LAL assay)
* Conductivity, pH, osmolality

QC releases materials before they can be used in manufacturing.

---

# 2️⃣ In-Process Testing (IPQC)


### Tests

* Cell viability
* Cell density
* Metabolites (glucose, lactate)
* pH, dissolved oxygen
* Protein titer (HPLC, ELISA)

---

## Downstream (Purification Monitoring)

### Tests

* Protein concentration
* Aggregation level
* Host cell protein (HCP)
* Host cell DNA
* Endotoxin

These tests ensure purification steps are working properly.

---

# 3️⃣ Final Product Testing (Drug Substance / Drug Product QC)

This is the most critical QC function.

## Identity Testing

* Peptide mapping (LC-MS)
* SDS-PAGE
* Western blot

## Purity & Impurity Profile
* Size Exclusion Chromatography (SEC) → aggregates
* Capillary electrophoresis
* SDS-PAGE
* RP-HPLC

---

## Potency Testing

* Cell-based bioassays
* Binding assays (ELISA, SPR)
* Functional activity assays

Potency assays are often the most complex and variable.

---

## Safety Testing

* Sterility (14-day culture)
* Endotoxin (LAL)
* Mycoplasma
* Bioburden

For injectable biologics, sterility is mandatory.

---

# 4️⃣ Stability Testing

QC runs stability programs under ICH guidelines:

* Accelerated (40°C)
* Long-term (2–8°C)
* Freeze-thaw studies

They monitor:

* Aggregation
* Potency loss
* Degradation
* Appearance
* pH changes

---

# 5️⃣ Documentation & Compliance

Everything QC does must comply with:

* **cGMP**
* **ICH guidelines**
* Regulatory agencies like

  * U.S. Food and Drug Administration
  * European Medicines Agency

### QC Responsibilities:

* SOP creation
* Method validation
* Deviation investigation
* OOS (Out-of-Specification) handling
* Audit readiness
* Data integrity (ALCOA+ principles)

---

# 6️⃣ Method Development & Validation Support

QC often works with Analytical Development to:

* Transfer validated methods
* Qualify equipment
* Validate analytical methods (accuracy, precision, robustness, specificity, linearity)

---

# 7️⃣ Environmental Monitoring (for sterile facilities)


* Air particle counts
* Settle plates
* Surface swabs
* Glove prints

Critical for aseptic manufacturing.

---

# 8️⃣ Release Decision Workflow

The QC flow typically looks like this:

1. Sample received
2. Logged into LIMS
3. Testing performed
4. Data reviewed
5. Second-person review
6. QA approval
7. Batch released

QC generates the analytical data; QA makes final release decisions.
